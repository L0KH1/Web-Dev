Principles & Culture

Embrace the Devil

A strength of GenCap lies within its varying and equally weighted perspectives. We will rarely agree fully as a team and an individual will often be wrong. Assuming potential error is essential to avoiding actual error. A GenCap member will always assume that his/her own assessments, along with those of others, could be incorrect or misinformed. Devil’s advocacy will be a major pillar of GenCap’s success. We must agree to disagree to agree.

Taste the Pizza

Peter Lynch’s “know what you own and know why you own it” is bluntly yet beautifully paraphrased by the legend himself, Bobby Axelrod, who said, “Whenever you can, put a company in your mouth.” Taste the pizza before you buy the restaurant. Simply put, whimsical or mob-mentality investments are off the table. A company may sound, look, and feel promising. That’s great, but how does it taste? Sure, every GenCap position is thoroughly considered through fundamentals, price, and valuation metrics. But we never stop there. An individual member brings a buy-candidate out to dinner and the voting committee takes it to bed. Knowledge is power. When we know and embrace a company, only then will it belong in our portfolio.

Don't Be Sheep

No GenCap investment is made on a whim. We always stick to our strategies and principles, only considering an investment when the data supports our hypothesis. An integral part of any investment decision at GenCap is the equal voting power of each member. Combining the perspectives and opinions of our member base provides a strict and methodic process for approving any decision.

Remain Transparent

Speak your mind and never be a “yes man.” As mentioned, we will often be wrong as individuals. The feelings of an individual member are much less important than the success of our team. Many legendary leaders (Jobs and Dalio, for example) are famous for creating environments of utter transparency no matter the social costs. We will do the same. Spoken opposition to an investment, managerial decision, task, etc. is not just invited but required. All members possess the same voice. Use it.

Prepare for All Outcomes

The global macro environment can change at an instant and requires an investor to cover all bases. In preparing for all outcomes, our investment strategies can easily pivot and are never concrete. Our portfolio is fueled by data and systemized by computer programming. We continuously prepare ourselves for the unexpected and seamlessly adjust our positions as needed. Preparing for all outcomes allows GenCap to embrace a long-term approach while benefitting from changes in the short-term environment.

Read Vivaciously

GenCap members educate themselves with past and current literature. Articles, books, and reports are shared within the group and discussed in depth. Whether we agree or disagree with a reading, all information within our scope is used to consider perspectives beyond our member base. We can always learn more. Like an investment compounds, so, too, does knowledge.

Consider the Bees

Seldom do we stop and listen to our planet’s most important living creature, the bee. Instead, media, markets, and smartphones seem to buzz louder. We eat foods and admire plants that would cease to exist without pollinators. Like this proverbial bee, the aggregate consumer and worker fuels our economy. Headlines may fuel volatility in the short run but, in the long run, it is individuals who are both the contributors to and reflections of our macro environments. An investor should not take for granted what hides in plain sight; the brands worn, foods eaten, services desired, and products used by consumers, among other observations, are early indicators. A GenCap member considers macro in light of observable micro. Noticing the small things allows one to not only understand but also predict the large things. Paying careful attention to the world and people around us is paramount. Always consider the bees.
